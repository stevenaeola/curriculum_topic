Students are expected to attend all lectures and workshops.

Students are expected to provide evidence of engagement with online materials by using the module discussion board for regular reflection on learning.

  • Multi-tier architectures.
  • Software design best practices and design patterns.
  • Security risks, threats and vulnerabilities (for example buffer overflows, integer overflows and race conditions) to systems as well as security standards.
  • Computer ethics and the law in relation to information systems.
  • Common pitfalls and mitigations in software engineering. 

  • To be able to develop software, we first need to understand some of the theories and approaches that will need to be applied in subsequent modules. This module will enable you to understand software architecture, security, and the law that governs information systems.

  • Tutor directed and self-directed study.
  • Lectures, workshops and seminars.
  • Online learning as directed.
  • Demonstrations.
  • Formative feedback on weekly set tasks and activities is given during face to face, by email and via the Discussion Board throughout the course.

  1. Understand Software Engineering architecture and the influences of business factors.
  2. Identify common pitfalls in Software Engineering implementation.
  3. Discuss the importance of information systems security.
  4. Identify areas where legislation impacts the software design process.
