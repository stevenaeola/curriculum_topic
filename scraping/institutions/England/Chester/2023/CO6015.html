Students are expected to arrange meetings with team colleagues as required by group-work activities.

The content will be delivered to students by directed study in tutorials and seminars. 

Self-directed study will take place when the students are creating multimedia objects throughout the module for their assessed project. 

On completion of this module the student will be able to -

1. Critically appraise new developments in specialist hardware and software relevant to games developments.

2. Present conclusions coherently within the context of a suitable theoretical framework.

3. To manage their own learning and make use of scholarly reviews and primary sources.

4. Accurately use a range of established techniques to implement computer games.

5. Be able to programme advanced gameplay and functionality within a modern game engine.

6. Apply methods and techniques previously learned to extend and apply their knowledge and understanding.

Assignment 1 consists of an individual presentation and poster that critically evaluates an area of current research and/or programming practice in the games industry.

Assignment 2 consists of the development of a game project in a games engine using advanced programming techniques to enhance functionality and gameplay.

Reassessment of any component will take the same form as the original assessment.
