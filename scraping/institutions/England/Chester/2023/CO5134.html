Students are expected to attend all lectures, tutorials and workshops.

Students are expected to provide evidence of engagement with online materials by using the Module Discussion Board for regular reflection on learning.

Students are expected to arrange meetings with team colleagues as required by group-work activities.

  1. Further 3D content techniques
    • Further 3D modelling and planning techniques
    • Additional UV unwrapping techniques
    • Texturing assets using a PBR workflow
    • Scene composition theory and techniques
    • Environmental storytelling techniques
    • New modelling concepts and techniques such as architectural modular kits
  1. Game Engine Integration
    • Importing, organising, and utilising content created in 3D software in an industry standard game engine
    • Materials and shaders
    • Additional tools and engine techniques
    • Lighting
    • Optimising environments

To introduce effective context storytelling as a fundamental component of modern games development. 
To introduce the student to the practical application of scene composition and environmental design.
To develop skills in creating interactive games environments for effective player experiences.
To further develop applied knowledge of a modern games development engine with focus on integration of assets.

Tutor-directed and self-directed study. Lectures, workshops and seminars. On-line learning as directed.

Formative feedback on weekly-set tasks and activities is given face-to-face and by email and discussion board.

  1. Understand and explain the importance of scene composition within 3D game environments.
  2. Develop skills in the design and development of an optimized game environment.
  3. Provide evidence of applied knowledge of environmental storytelling within a games context.
  4. Demonstrate skills in advanced 3D modelling and assets creation.
  5. Demonstrate proficiency with a 3D game engine in line with industry standard techniques.
