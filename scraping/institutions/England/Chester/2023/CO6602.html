  • Students are expected to attend all lectures, tutorials and workshops.

  • Public/private key composition and usage
  • Certificates – for code signing and identify management
  • Block/stream ciphers
  • Cryptographic algorithms
  • History of and modern day cryptography
  • Modern day tools for enterprise and personal use for protection of data and relevant confidential information
  • Encryption attacks (e.g. Ransomware) and attacks against encryption (anti-encryption)

  • To give an understanding and usage of symmetric/asymmetric encryption
  • To explore basic cryptographic calculations
  • To understand how cryptographic algorithms work
  • To give an appreciation to system and data security using cryptography
  • To explore case studies and advise on the right actions to take to ensure continued data protection
  • To explore the history of cryptography and cryptographic attacks

  • Tutorials.
  • Practical task oriented and possible group work.
  • Peer reviews.
  • Tutor directed and self-directed study.
  • Online learning as directed.
  • Demonstrations and practical exercises.
  • Formative feedback will be given during contact time, by email and via discussion boards where appropriate.

  1. Perform some basic cryptographic calculations
  2. Critically measure and differentiate between how encryption/decryption works from an applied cryptography point of view
  3. Demonstrate an understanding of security certificates, digital signatures, hash functions, and their uses
  4. Analyse historical and current ciphers and demonstrate relevant types suitable for achieving a secure platform
  5. Formulate security practices that an organisation should use to deal with cryptographic attacks and to stop theft of data on endpoint devices. This will include how to make attack impracticable and security practices relating to access control principles - e.g. Least Privilege, Single Sing-On, NFA (N Factor Authentication), etc.
  6. Demonstrate a good understanding of the design and internal structures of commonly used cryptosystems

Essential Reading:

  • Paar, C. and Pelzl, J. (2010). Understanding Cryptography: A Textbook for Students and Practitioners. Springer.
  • Aumasson, J. (2017). Serious Cryptography: A Practical Introduction to Modern Encryption. No starch press.

Recommended Reading:

  • Dumas, J., Roch, J-L., Tannier, E., & Varrette, S. (2015). Foundations of coding: Compression, encryption, error correction. Hoboken, NJ: Wiley-Blackwell.
  • Stallings, W. (2016). Cryptography and network security: Principles and practice. (7th Ed.). Boston, MA: Pearson.
  • Sweigart, A. (2013). Hacking secret ciphers with python: A beginner's guide to cryptography and computer programming with Python. North Charleston, SC: CreateSpace.
  • Azad, S. & Khan-Pathan, A. (2015). Practical cryptography: Algorithms and implementations using C++. Boca Raton, FL: CRC Press.

In addition a range of relevant research articles available through ACM Digital Library, IEEE/IET Electronic Library and other online journals should be used.
