Ongoing engagement with the module

Students are expected to provide evidence of engagement with online materials by using the module discussion board and workshops opportunities, for regular reflection on learning.

  • Defining user experience design and the various subsets; including usability.
  • An introduction to human factors and human-centred design standards.
  • History of HCI and issues surrounding technology and innovation.
  • Practical design and prototyping of user interface solutions to solve 'real world' problems.
  • Experimenting with iterative team-based design and development exercises covering insights gathering, design processes, industry-approved frameworks, and user testing and evaluation.
  • Introduction to careers in UX.

  • To encourage critical reflection on the needs of humans as users/consumers, and the demands and relevant constraints imposed.
  • To ensure awareness of the rate of technological change, the effects on users/consumers, and the issues relevant to user experience designers.
  • To promote inquiry into current leading digital technologies and possible future developments in ubiquitous computing.
  • To give practice in the design and prototyping of user experiences and user interfaces, especially for web-based, console, desktop systems, and mobile devices.
  • Gain "real-world" experience of taking part as a team member in a software development project.

  • Tutor directed and self directed study. Lectures, workshops/tutorials and seminars.
  • Online learning as directed, plus demonstrations & practical exercises.
  • Formative feedback will be given during contact time, by email and via virtual learning environments where appropriate.

  1. Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of key principles in the development of human-centered systems. 
  2. Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of techniques associated with the thoughtful design of user interfaces.
  3. Select ‘fit for purpose’ methods and techniques for capturing, transferring and presenting data.
  4. Produce team-based, design-focused and human-centred solutions towards the creation of an interactive system prototype.
  5. Understand and reflect upon the multidisciplinary nature and significance of roles within a software development team.
  6. Evaluate a prototype system using the appropriate tools and methodology.

Assessment (team-based coursework) assesses the student on the meaningful practical application of the module theory (LOs 1-3), towards an interactive software prototype with evaluation (LOs 4-6). Evaluation involves a team-based oral presentation covering a detailed rationale of the theory and design-development process.
Reassessment will be in the same format as the original assessment, with a narrated presentation replacing any oral face-to-face presentation.
