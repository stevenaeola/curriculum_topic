Due to the importance of laying the required foundations in the study of cybersecurity, this module requires that students attend all timetabled sessions.

Students are expected to provide evidence of engagement with online materials by using the Module Discussion Board for regular reflection on learning.

An introduction to the Cybersecurity realm:

  • Foundations of computer systems, software, and networking, with respect to the Cybersecurity perspective
  • Threats, resolutions, and auditing, relating to IT systems and governance
  • Speaking Cyber - Core understanding of Cybersecurity terminologies
  • Implementing secure systems. Illustrative of what may be covered include:
    • Design and development considerations
    • Selecting and applying core technologies
    • Cryptography
    • Human factors
    • Auditing (security audits, logging, digital investigation etc.)
    • Defense-in-depth
    • Pentesting
    • Access control

  • To give a solid whole-picture foundation which can then be harnessed later in CO5606 and CO5607 where the modules build upon challenging concepts
  • To empower the students with core understanding of the key terminologies in cybersecurity
  • To provide students with the understanding of the common cybersecurity concepts and technical security controls available to prevent, detect and recover from security incidents

  • Lectures and tutorials.
  • Peer reviews.
  • Tutor directed and self-directed study.
  • Online learning as directed.
  • Demonstrations, practical, and conceptual exercises.
  • Group work.
  • Formative feedback will be given during contact time, by email and via discussion boards where appropriate.

  1. Show clear understanding of key cybersecurity terminologies.
  2. Discuss common technical weaknesses and security measures.
  3. Demonstrate a conceptual understanding of threats and resolutions with respect to Information and System Security.
  4. Demonstrate understanding of knowledge, and a critical awareness of cybersecurity core concepts and techniques.

Essential reading

  • Solomon, M., & Chapple, M. (2005). Information Security Illuminated. Jones and Bartlett.
  • Alexander, D., Finch A., Sutton, D., & Taylor, A. (2013). Information Security Management Principles. (2nd Rev. Ed.) BCS, The Chartered Institute for IT.
  • Weidman, G. (2014). Penetration Testing – A Hands-On Introduction to Hacking. No Starch Press.

Recommended reading

  • Erickson, J. (2008). Hacking – The Art of Exploitation. (2nd Ed.) No Starch Press.
  • Wu, C. & Irwin, J. D. (2013). Introduction to Computer Networks and Cybersecurity. CRC Press.
  • Rao, U., & Nayak, U. (2014). The Infosec Handbook: An Introduction to Information Security. Apress.

An interesting read

  • Mitnick, K. (2003). The Art of Deception: Controlling the Human Element of Security. John Wiley & Sons.
  • Mitnick, K. D. & Simon, W. L. (2005). The Art of Intrusion – The Real Stories Behind the Exploits of Hackers, Intruders and Deceivers. John Wiley & Sons.
  • Mitnick, K. & Simon, W. L. (2012). Ghost In The Wires: My Adventures as the World's Most Wanted Hacker. Back Bay.

In addition a range of relevant research articles available through ACM Digital Library, IEEE/IET Electronic Library and other online journals should be used.
