As this module incorporates assessed group work, evidence of at least 75% of individual engagement with the team and process is required. Tutors monitor live attendance via online collaborative tools utilised in the module for each scheduled session. This will be made clear to all students at the start of the module.

If the engagement requirement is not met by any individual student, the mark for that person will be capped at 40%.

It is the responsibility of individual students to make sure their engagement is sufficient. Due to the importance of setting out clear roles and responsibilities from the outset, any negative influence of one individual will not affect other team members' potential to achieve.

  • Balancing business goals with user needs
  • UX research
  • Experience mapping
  • Design thinking

  • Progressive enhancement
  • User interface design techniques
  • Defining inclusive design and accessibility
  • Style-guides and specifications for developers
  • Design sprints for software innovation
  • User testing and evaluation

  • To demonstrate effective use of human-centred design techniques according to ISO 9241-210 standard
  • To develop a solid appreciation of the importance of UX research in product teams
  • To design effective information architecture for interactive systems
  • To explore diverse user interface design and interaction design issues for the benefit of the user experience
  • To gain first-hand experience in collaborative software innovation workshops
  • To develop a solid understanding of the dynamic and multidisciplinary nature of software development teams
  • To appreciate the importance of designing for inclusivity and a diverse range of users
  • To undertake user testing and evaluation of interactive systems

  • Directed and Self-Directed Study.
  • Lectures, workshops/tutorials and seminars.
  • Online learning as directed.
  • Individual demonstrations and practical exercises.
  • Team-based demonstrations and practical exercises.
  • Formative feedback will be given during contact time, by email and via discussion boards where appropriate.

  1. Critically analyse user experience issues in technology and software innovation.
  2. Demonstrate a good understanding of the human-centred design lifecycle, according to ISO 9241-210.
  3. Demonstrate advanced teamwork skills in a collaborative software development environment.
  4. Use insights to inform the development of personas, user journeys and system workflows, to ensure user and organisational needs are met.
  5. Apply parts of the ISO 9241 standard to the development of original software.
  6. Show an awareness of inclusive design issues software innovation.

Two individual projects (Assignment 1 - written report worth 25% of the module and Assignment 2 - practical assignment worth 75%) providing evidence of advanced skills in applying contemporary standards, UX research, design principles, prototyping techniques, and the evaluation of an interactive system.
Reassessment of Assignment 1 will take the same form as the original assessment.

Reassessment of Assignment 2 will involve a 3000 word written report on topics pertinent to LO3, LO4, LO5.
