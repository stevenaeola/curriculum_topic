Students are expected to attend all lectures, tutorials, workshops, and seminars, as well as engage in the online (VLE) forum.

Students are expected to provide evidence of engagement with online materials by using the Module Discussion Board for regular reflection on learning.

This module uses the traffic-light-system technique for group work. This technique allows members of a group to issue an initial warning (amber-signal) to a non-performing group member and a final warning (red-signal) when the member fails to improve. If the erring member fails to improve, after an intervention by the tutor and without a proof of mitigating circumstance, and a red-signal is issued, the student automatically loses 50% of the group's grade. A group member who fails to attend the group presentation without an approved extension/deferral will lose the entire presentation mark. Anyone who doesnâ€™t attend the presentation for approved reasons will be invited to attend a viva to explain their contribution to the group work.

Both the initial warning (amber-signal) and the final warning (red-signal) MUST be by email to the member in question. All group members and the tutor MUST be copied into the email. The deadline for raising any complaint is 5 working days before assignment deadline. Even if a traffic light warning is not triggered in a group, the tutor may use viva voce as explained in the Academic Integrity Policy, meaning that individuals may be invited before a panel to explain their contribution to the group work.

The group pairings are set by the lecturer and are non-negotiable. Students are expected, where necessary, to arrange meetings with team colleagues and be active members as required by group-work activities.

Component 1: This is an individual technical report on selected topics covering parts or all of Learning Outcomes 1, 2, and 3.

Component 2: This is a group work with two deliverables, including a group report and a group presentation, as designated in the Assignment Brief. Students are expected to work in groups of 3-5 to produce a written report and present their report via a pre-prepared format such as PowerPoint Presentation with YouTube video. This assignment covers parts or all of Learning Outcomes 3, 4, and 5.

Each group is to appoint one member who will submit on behalf of everyone in accordance with details given in the Assignment Brief.

The traffic-light-system technique (explained in the Attendance section) will be applied to the group work.

Reassessment of any component will take the same form as the original assessment.

  • Ligh, M. H. (2014). The Art of Memory Forensics: Detecting Malware and Threats in Windows, Linux, and Mac Memory. Wiley.
