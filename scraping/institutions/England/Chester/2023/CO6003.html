This module equips the student with a capacity to develop strategic approaches to problems requiring a technological solution, as well as operational policies within an organisation that can help to improve and protect its competitive effectiveness.

The module's aims are:

  • To provide the student with the skills needed to examine and critically analyse policies for technological change and innovation within an organisation. 
  • To provide the student with the capacity to critically assess the role of ICT in effective management.

Directed and Self Directed Study, Open Learning, Tutorials, Lectures, Case studies.

 On completion of this module, the student will be able to -

  1. Define and analyse the significant aspects of technological innovation.
  2. Analyse and evaluate the application of hardware and software, along with its uses, both local and global, in society and in organisations.
  3. Devise and sustain arguments using a wide range of ideas and techniques within the field of technical innovation strategy.
  4. Exercise judgement in a number of complex performance, planning, design and technical functions related to the devising and validation of a model for technical innovation.
  5. Synthesis and integrate information from a variety of sources.
  6. Critically comprehend and assess the relationship between current practices in the area of computing, and an appropriate application of technological and ethical responsibility.
