A good mathematical grounding is essential for all computing scientists. The mathematics in this syllabus provides the foundation for deeper analysis and understanding of computing subjects, and enhances the student’s ability to make sound judgements regarding the appropriate technology that they will use in the course of their career.  Wherever possible, mathematical theory is taught by considering a real example, to provide students with the context for the mathematical tools they will use.  Solutions are considered by both analytical and numerical techniques. Where basic principles are involved, some proofs will also be taught.

The total student effort for this module is 100 hours.  This total includes:

Lectures, where they are the most efficient way of imparting required knowledge in this context. Students will be directed towards recommended reading materials and online resources;

Tutorials (associated with lectures) provide opportunities to reinforce student’s knowledge and for formative assessment. Students are encouraged to attempt worksheets in advance of the session and to be prepared to ask and answer questions relating to the material;

Formative assessment in tutorials trains the student in problem solving.

The remaining time is allocated for student-centred learning for exam preparation.

  1. Demonstrate knowledge and understanding of functions, calculus, vectors, linear algebra complex numbers, and ordinary differential equations.
  2. Select and apply the appropriate mathematical techniques to solve algorithmic problems using clear, logical arguments and appropriate mathematical language.
  3. Evaluate and use relevant data in the solution of a problem.
