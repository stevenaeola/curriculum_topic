Scheduled hours Placement Hours Independent Guided study
40 0 160

Students are expected to attend all lectures, tutorials and workshops.

  • Client/server side scripting and coding
  • Full-stack web application development and configuration
  • Web application frameworks
  • User-interface software design patterns
  • Thin and thick client architectures
  • Database-driven websites and database connectivity

  • To introduce concepts of web-application development and configuration
  • To extend and enhance students' knowledge of current web technologies.
  • To develop student' proficiency and confidence in applying theoretical and practical methodologies for the design, implementation, evaluation of and management of web-based systems.
  • To introduce the use of web-application frameworks towards the development of interactive web applications

  • Tutor directed and self-directed study.
  • Lectures, workshops/tutorials.
  • Online learning as directed. Demonstrations and practical exercises.
  • Formative feedback will be given during contact time, by email and via discussion boards where appropriate.

  1. Demonstrate an understanding of the issues surrounding the design, deployment and operation of web applications.
  2. Apply appropriate techniques for the deployment, management and maintenance of web applications.
  3. Reflect on the reasons why some systems are more successful than others, and make reasoned arguments as to the extent to which this is influenced by usability.
  4. Assess the role of web frameworks in web application development.
