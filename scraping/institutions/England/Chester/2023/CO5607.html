Students are expected to attend all lectures, tutorials, workshops, and seminars, as well as engage in the online (VLE) forum.

Students are expected to provide evidence of engagement with online materials by using the Module Discussion Board for regular reflection on learning.

  • Software attack techniques, approaches, and methodologies
  • Exposure to exploitation techniques, including for example, buffer overflow, stack overflow, heap attacks, and UAF (Use After Free), amongst others.
  • Attack methods including system, host, network, and web-based attacks.
  • Social engineering awareness and mitigation
  • Network security and attacks
  • Use of penetration testing tools
  • Network tapping/sniffing and detection
  • Wireless and mobile hacking
  • Ethical issues in software exploitation and penetrations testing

  • To understand basic assembly code of a given program
  • To practice previously acquired skills in theory and apply them using software tools
  • To plan and develop attacks on systems
  • To understand the inner working of common technologies and demonstrate the flaws that exist
  • To understand network attacks and security, and to be able to critique the security of a network

  • Tutorials.
  • Practical task oriented group work.
  • Peer reviews.
  • Tutor directed and self-directed study.
  • Online learning as directed.
  • Demonstrations and practical exercises.
  • Formative feedback will be given during contact time, by email and via discussion boards where appropriate.

  1. Understand and demonstrate software exploitation techniques
  2. Demonstrate an understanding of counter-measures against exploitation techniques
  3. Demonstrate systematic understanding of secure system design and software protection methods
  4. Demonstrate the correct use of tools and techniques in a successful penetration test
  5. Understand ways of gaining access to networks and performing monitoring on networks
  6. Perform penetration testing on networks and systems, identifying vulnerable systems
  7. Understanding the weaknesses in networks and software to mitigate attack and exploitation

Component 1: This will comprise a portfolio of practical exercises carried out over a minimum of 6 weeks. Each week will be submitted within an allocated time for that week’s activity such that continuous feedback can be provided for improvement. The portfolio will then be assessed in whole at the end. This assignment covers parts or all of Learning Outcomes 1, 2, 3, and 7.

Component 2: This is an individual technical report covering parts or all of Learning Outcomes 4, 5, 6, and 7.

Reassessment of Component 1 may take an alternative (though equivalent) mode. This will match the original assessment in weightings and in Learning Outcomes addressed.

Reassessment of Component 2 will take the same form as the original assessment.
